How to turn on javascript and cookies
 

How do I enable JavaScript in Internet Explorer 6.x or 7.x?

To enable JavaScript in Microsoft Internet Explorer 6x or 7.x, perform the following steps:

From the Tools menu, click Internet Options.


From the Security tab, click Custom Level.

The Internet icon is highlighted by default.


Scroll to Java permissions, click to select High safety.


Click OK.

Click OK.


From the File menu, click Close.


Relaunch your browser.

Your browser stores many of its settings. The best way to ensure changes to core function settings to take effect is to close all browser windows completely, then relaunch it.

 

How do I enable Cookies in Internet Explorer 6.x or 7.x?

Cookies are defined as messages given to a web browser by a web server. The browser stores the messages in a text files called cookie.txt. The message is then sent back to the server each time the browser requests the page from the server. The main purpose of cookies is to identify users.

To enable cookies in Internet Explorer 6.x or 7.x, perform the following steps:

From the Tools menu, click Internet Options.


From the Privacy tab, click Advanced.


To adjust the settings, click to select Override automatic cookie handling.


Click to choose Accept, Block or Prompt. Click OK.


First party cookies come from the site you are visiting, for example Yahoo.

Third party cookies come from a third party who has permission from the site you are visiting to send cookies to you. For example, Yahoo allows Casino.com to send cookies to your computer.

Click OK.
